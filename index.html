Background 03

I'm 18, from Mumbai. My father built a B2B electrical distribution business from scratch — Shriman Electricals — and I grew up watching how traditional trade actually works: the margins, the supplier relationships, the chaos of inventory that never quite maps to demand. That proximity to a real, unglamorous business is the lens through which I think about technology.

I started early. GPU mining at 13. A Chia pivot when the economics shifted. Live capital trading across Indian equities, built decision frameworks around RSI, EMA, and macro signals. Eventually wrote API-based market monitoring systems on Zerodha and Groww. None of it was academic. All of it had skin in the game.

I'm now studying Computer Science at BITS Pilani, Goa — on a dual-degree track to CentraleSupélec Paris for years three and four, where I'll specialise in AI. The path is deliberate: technical depth in Europe, market intuition built at home, and a thesis about the 63 million Indian distributors still waiting for their infrastructure moment.
